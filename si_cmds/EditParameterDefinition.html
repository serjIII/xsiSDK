EditParameterDefinition

EditParameterDefinition

Introduced

v1.5

Description

Edits the definition of a custom parameter. Skipped arguments are left unchanged.

For proxy parameters it is possible to adjust the parameter name and suggested range using this command, which leaves the master parameter unaffected.

It is also possible to adjust proxy parameters capabilities, which lead to also update the master parameters capabilities. However the Min and Max attributes of a proxy parameter should be left the same as the master parameter's Min and Max values.

Scripting Syntax

EditParameterDefinition( [InputObj], [ParamName], [VarType], [MinValue], [MaxValue], [SuggMin], [SuggMax], [ScriptName], [Description], [Capabilities] );

Parameters

Parameter Type Description
InputObj String List of custom parameters.

Default Value: Marked parameters

ParamName String Name of the custom parameter. This appears on the property page when the object is inspected and in the scene explorer (unless "Show script names" is enabled).
VarType siVariantType (Obsolete) It is not necessary to specify this argument, it is ignored.

Default Value: 0

MinValue Double Minimum value of the parameter
MaxValue Double Maximum value of the parameter
SuggMin Double Suggested minimum value of the parameter (for UI controls)
SuggMax Double Suggested maximum value of the parameter (for UI controls)
ScriptName String Scripting version of the parameter name. There should not be any space characters in the string.
Description String A longer, descriptive version of the parameter name.
Capabilities siCapabilities Determines the capabilities of the parameter (see the siCapabilities enum for more details)

Examples

VBScript Example

'
' This example creates a new custom property set and a new
' parameter on a dummy null and then changes its definition.
' The parameter information is logged to the Script Editor
' History Pane so you can see what has changed.
'
' Create a null to hold the new custom property set
set oDummy = GetPrim( "Null" )
' Add custom property set called Emotions to the dummy
AddProp "Custom_parameter_list", , , "Emotions"
' Add custom parameter Happiness to the Emotions property set
SIAddCustomParameter "null.Emotions", "Happiness", _
			siDouble, 0.000, 0.000, 1.000, , siAnimatable, 0.000, 1.000, , "The happiness param"
' What does it look like before editing the definition?
printParamInfo oDummy.Properties( "Emotions" ).Parameters(0)
' Update the minimum and maximum values of Happiness
EditParameterDefinition oDummy & ".Emotions.Happiness", "Sadness Param", , _
			-100, 100, -100, 100, "Sadness", "The sadness param", _
			siAnimatable + siKeyable
' What does it look like afterwards?
printParamInfo oDummy.Properties( "Emotions" ).Parameters(0)
' Convenience function--saves time and typing
function printParamInfo( in_parameter )
	LogMessage in_parameter & " information:"
	LogMessage "-------------------------------------"
	LogMessage vbTab & "Default value: " & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Default
	LogMessage vbTab & "Description: " & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Description
	LogMessage vbTab & "Is " & in_parameter.ScriptName & " marked?: " & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Marked
	LogMessage vbTab & "SuggestedMin value: " & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.SuggestedMin
	LogMessage vbTab & "SuggestedMax value: " & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.SuggestedMax
	LogMessage vbTab & "Actual Min value: " & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Min
	LogMessage vbTab & "Actual Max value: " & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Max
	LogMessage vbTab & "ScriptName: " & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.ScriptName
	LogMessage vbTab & "Name: " & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Name
	LogMessage vbTab & "Capabilities: " & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Capabilities
	LogMessage vbTab & "Is " & in_parameter.ScriptName & " animatable?: " & vbTab & in_parameter.Animatable
	LogMessage vbTab & "Is " & in_parameter.ScriptName & " keyable?: " & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Keyable
	LogMessage vbTab & "Is " & in_parameter.ScriptName & " read-only?:  " & vbTab & in_parameter.ReadOnly
	LogMessage vbTab & "Value Type: " & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.ValueType & " (" & TypeName( in_parameter ) & ")"
	LogMessage vbTab & "Actual Value: " & vbTab & vbTab & vbTab & in_parameter.Value
end function
